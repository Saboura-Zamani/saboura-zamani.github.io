Quantum Software lab
Uni. of Edinburgh, 2024-Present

I am a PhD researcher at the Quantum Software Lab, where I work on distributed quantum computing. My focus is on designing and evaluating architectures for distributing quantum computations across networked quantum devices. This includes benchmarking cross-platform setups using simulated and real quantum hardware. I develop tools to translate monolithic circuits into distributed workloads, model communication costs, and analyze the trade-offs between locality, parallelism, and reliability. The goal is to understand what quantum tasks are best suited for offloading and how to coordinate them efficiently over constrained quantum networks.

National Quantum Computing Centre
UK Research and Innovation, March-May 2025

As a student resident researcher at the NQCC, I focused on quantum programming and its industrial applications. During the program, I co-developed a quantum simulation of the Vlasov–Poisson equation, a key model in plasma physics, using real quantum hardware (Rigetti). The project implemented time evolution via Trotterization and evaluated the potential of near-term quantum devices for solving kinetic equations. Working with senior quantum application engineers, I adapted scientific workloads to quantum platforms and benchmarked their performance against classical solvers.

bqb Quantum Youth, 2022-2026

As the Director of bqb Quantum Youth, I co-founded and built an international team of students from diverse academic backgrounds to create and deliver educational content for high school students. Ingenii
Ingenii, 2023

I contributed to Ingenii's quantum error mitigation stack. My library included existing error mitigation codes such as Clifford Data Regression, Neural Error Mitigation, and Measurement Error Mitigation, along with a comparison algorithm that allows users to identify the ideal error mitigation program for their specific project—improving users' accuracy while saving time and resources in implementing the optimal technique. The library's testing and implementation were carried out using Ingenii data and experiments sourced from academic collaborations.
